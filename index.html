Legal Framework

Rules, Regulations & Compliance

India's real estate regulatory environment is multi-layered. Clarity here is the difference between a clean transaction and a decade-long legal headache. We break it down by investor type.

For Resident Indian Buyers

What does RERA Maharashtra mean for me as a buyer?
RERA mandates that every new residential project above 500 sqm or 8 units must be registered. The developer must park 70% of collections in an escrow account, provide quarterly construction updates, and deliver on the registered floor plan. If they deviate, you can approach MahaRERA for refund with interest.
What is the stamp duty and registration cost in Mumbai?
Stamp duty in Mumbai is 5% for men and 4% for women on property value. Metro cess adds 1%, and a local body tax of 1% applies. Registration charges are 1% of property value up to ‚Çπ30 lakh. On a ‚Çπ2 crore property, total cost is approximately ‚Çπ14‚Äì16 lakhs.
When does GST apply and at what rate?
GST applies only to under-construction properties ‚Äî 5% for non-affordable and 1% for affordable housing. Ready-to-move properties with OC (Occupancy Certificate) attract zero GST. This makes OC-received properties significantly more cost-efficient for buyers.
What are the critical red flags before signing an agreement?
Verify RERA registration on MahaRERA website, check if OC/CC has been received for completed projects, confirm clear title through a registered advocate's title search, verify no encumbrances in EC (Encumbrance Certificate), and ensure the floor plan matches RERA-registered drawings.

For NRI Investors (Indians Abroad)

Can NRIs buy property in Mumbai freely?
Yes. NRIs (Non-Resident Indians) and PIOs (Persons of Indian Origin) can purchase any number of residential and commercial properties in India without RBI approval. The only restriction is on agricultural land, plantation property, and farmhouses ‚Äî which require special government approval.
Which account should funds flow through?
Purchase funds must flow through NRE (Non-Resident External) or NRO (Non-Resident Ordinary) accounts, or through foreign currency remittance via normal banking channels. Cash transactions are prohibited. Using NRE ensures full repatriation rights on both principal and gains.
How does TDS work when an NRI sells property?
The buyer of an NRI-owned property must deduct TDS at 20% (for long-term gains, held 2+ years) or 30% (short-term) at the time of payment. NRIs can apply for a Lower Deduction Certificate from their Income Tax officer to reduce this rate based on actual gains, which significantly improves cash flow.
Can I repatriate my sale proceeds abroad?
Yes, up to USD 1 million per financial year can be repatriated from NRO account after paying applicable taxes. Funds in NRE accounts are fully and freely repatriable. If you purchased via NRE funds, the entire capital plus gains can be repatriated without limit, subject to FEMA compliance documentation.

For Foreign Nationals (Non-Indian)

Can foreign nationals buy property in India?
Foreign nationals (non-NRI, non-PIO) can only acquire immovable property in India by way of inheritance or as a gift from an NRI/PIO relative. They cannot purchase residential property outright. However, they can lease property for periods up to 5 years without RBI approval.
What commercial real estate structures are available?
Foreign entities can invest in Indian commercial real estate through REITs listed on Indian exchanges, FDI into real estate companies (under approved routes), or through a registered Indian entity. Commercial leasing is available under FEMA's liberalised guidelines with standard rental agreements.
Are there structures to access Mumbai real estate as a foreign fund?
Yes. Foreign Portfolio Investors (FPIs) can invest in listed REITs and InvITs. Foreign Venture Capital Investors (FVCIs) can access unlisted development companies. The AIF (Alternative Investment Fund) route registered with SEBI allows participation in real estate private equity structures that own Mumbai assets.
Should I consult a legal advisor before proceeding?
Absolutely. Foreign national investment into Indian real estate involves FEMA compliance, SEBI regulations, RBI approvals (in some cases), and income tax treaty analysis. Engaging a FEMA-specialist advocate and a CA experienced in cross-border investment is essential before any commitment of capital.
